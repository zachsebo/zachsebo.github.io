Zachary Sebo
Postdoc | Aging & Metabolism

Age is the single greatest risk factor for frailty and diseases ranging from cancer to Alzheimer's. The biological process of aging is scientifically interpretable and can be therapeutically modified. My research aims to contribute to the nascent field of gerotherapeutics, a term used to describe drugs that delay the onset of age-related decline. In particular, I study how small molecules that alter mitochondrial function elicit systemic health benefits. I also have a deep interest in the molecular basis of exercise adaptations.

I work with an excellent team of scientists led by Navdeep Chandel at Northwestern University.

My doctoral studies at Yale focused on obesity and type II diabetes.
